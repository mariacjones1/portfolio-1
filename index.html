Timeline

9 April 1483

Edward IV dies, making his 12-year-old son and heir King Edward V.

29 April 1483

Richard, Duke of Gloucester, takes possession of Edward V. Elizabeth Woodville and her remaining children claim sanctuary in Westminster Abbey.

4 May 1483

Edward's coronation is postponed from 4 May to 25 June.

19 May 1483

Edward is lodged in the Tower of London.

16 June 1483

Edward V is joined in the Tower by his brother Richard.

25 June 1483

Both princes are declared illegitimate.

6 July 1483

Gloucester is crowned King Richard III.

End of summer 1483

The princes are seen for the last time.

22 August 1485

Richard III is killed at the Battle of Bosworth Field. Henry VII becomes King.

1487

Lambert Simnel claims first to be the missing Prince Richard, then Edward, Earl of Warwick.

1490

Perkin Warbeck claims to be the missing Prince Richard. He is executed in 1499.

17 July 1674

Workmen find two small skeletons in the White Tower, assumed to be the missing princes. The are interred in Westminster Abbey.

1933

The bones are removed and examined. It is concluded that the bones belonged to two children of around the correct age for the princes.
